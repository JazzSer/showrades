# Market Research on competitors
Based on the unique vision of **Showrades**—a zero-friction, open-source, multi-generational web app for charades featuring a "Dual-Age Engine" (Pictures Mode with hidden clues for young kids + Words Mode for older players) and a sneaky-educational focus—here is a quick market research summary of existing platforms and how Showrades compares.

---

### 1. Existing Platforms & Competitors

#### **A. App-Based Charades & Guessing Games (High Friction, Pop-Culture Focused)**

* **Heads Up! (Warner Bros. / Ellen DeGeneres):** * *What it is:* The most well-known mobile guessing game where players hold the phone to their forehead and guess from friends' clues.
* *Limitations:* Requires App Store/Google Play downloads, shows ads/in-app purchases, and isn't optimized for mixed pre-reader/adult play within the same unified game board.


* **Charades! ™ / Charades & Headbands (FatChicken Studios / artGS):**
* *What it is:* Popular mobile apps replicating the "Heads Up" style with various themed decks.
* *Limitations:* Requires device installation, lacks offline capabilities for remote/camp areas, and does not have built-in educational/developmental framing.



#### **B. Browser-Based Party Games (No Downloads, but Limited Scope)**

* **Playmime.com / Who Am I? (playwhoami.com):**
* *What it is:* Web-based "Heads Up" style clones that require no installation or login.
* *Limitations:* Simple text/GIF-based word banks meant primarily for teens/adults. They lack illustration modes for pre-readers, parent-specific clue hints, and multi-generational scaling.


* **Skribbl.io / DoodleCharades:**
* *What it is:* Web-based drawing and guessing games.
* *Limitations:* Focused strictly on *drawing* (Pictionary) rather than physical pantomime/acting (Charades), and lacks an educational curriculum or kid-friendly, non-reading interfaces.



---

### 2. Market Gap & Positioning for Showrades

Almost all digital party games fall into two camps: **App store downloads** (which create friction when passing a phone to toddlers or older, less-tech-savvy grandparents) or **bare-bones web tools** (which lack polished content, visual accessibility, and family-friendly design).

**Showrades occupies a highly defensible, unique white-space:**

1. **The Dual-Age Engine:** No major digital charades platform offers a unified UI/scoring system where a 5-year-old gets full-screen illustrated prompts (with a discreet "Secret Clue" for parents) while an older sibling or grandparent gets crisp, large text.
2. **Zero-Friction Web App:** Running instantly in a mobile browser without any downloads, sign-ups, or invasive ads removes the technical barrier for multi-generational families.
3. **Sneaky-Educational Focus:** Unlike commercial apps built around pop-culture trivia or memes, Showrades is explicitly designed around developmental benefits (gross motor skills, biomimicry, emotional literacy, spatial awareness) making it highly attractive to parents and educators.
4. **Offline Resilience:** The ability to run offline (once loaded) makes it highly reliable for family gatherings, camping, or living rooms with unstable Wi-Fi.
